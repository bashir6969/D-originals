The Year, in Order

a totally accurate, slightly exaggerated timeline

September
First day. Nobody knew anybody.
Awkward seating chart energy. By week two the back row had already named itself and nobody else was invited.
established: the group chat
October
The infamous fire drill mid-quiz
Half the class left their papers behind. The other half tried to keep writing on the way out the door. A legend was born.
December
Finals week chaos arc
Caffeine, shared notes at 1am, and at least one person who definitely did not study but somehow pulled through anyway.
mvp: whoever shared the study guide
February
The substitute who let us pick the seating
One day of total anarchy. We talk about it more than we talk about actual class content.
April
Field trip / project season
Group projects tested friendships. Most survived. A few alliances were permanently reshuffled.
June
Last day. Somehow already here.
Yearbooks signed, plans made for summer, and the slow realization that next year won't have the exact same room, same seats, same chaos.
